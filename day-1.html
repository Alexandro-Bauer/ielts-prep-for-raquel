Hour 2: Listening Basics

The IELTS Listening test consists of four sections with increasing difficulty. Let's start with the basics.

Key Listening Skills:

  • Listening for specific information (names, numbers, dates)
  • Understanding instructions and question types
  • Note-taking techniques
  • Spelling accuracy (especially for names)

Hour 3: Reading Fundamentals

Let's start building your reading skills with skimming and scanning techniques.

Key Reading Strategies:

  • Skimming: Reading quickly to get the main idea
  • Scanning: Looking for specific information
  • Understanding paragraph structure
  • Identifying topic sentences

Practice Exercise: Skimming and Scanning

Read the following passage quickly (in 1 minute) and answer the questions:

Honey bees are social insects that live in colonies. A colony typically consists of three types of bees: the queen, the workers, and the drones. The queen's primary function is to reproduce; she lays all the eggs in the colony. Worker bees, which are all female, perform all the labour tasks in the hive. These include building and maintaining the honeycomb, collecting nectar and pollen, producing honey, and caring for the young. Drones are male bees whose primary function is to mate with the queen. They don't have stingers and don't gather nectar or pollen. Bees communicate with each other using a complex system of movements known as the "waggle dance." Through this dance, forager bees can tell other bees the location of food sources. The direction and duration of the dance indicate the direction and distance of the food source from the hive. Honey bees play a crucial role in agriculture through pollination. As they collect nectar and pollen from flowers, they transfer pollen from male to female flower parts, enabling plants to produce seeds and fruits. It's estimated that one-third of the food we eat depends on bee pollination.
  1. How many types of bees are in a colony?
  2. What is the "waggle dance" used for?
  3. What percentage of our food depends on bee pollination?
  4. Which type of bee is responsible for building the honeycomb?
  5. Do drones have stingers?
