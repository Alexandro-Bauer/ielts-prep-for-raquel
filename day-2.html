Hour 1: Speaking Introduction

The IELTS Speaking test is a face-to-face interview with an examiner that lasts 11-14 minutes. It is divided into three parts and tests your ability to communicate effectively in English.

IELTS Speaking Resources

Learn about the IELTS Speaking test format and preparation strategies:

  • British Council - Speaking Preparation
  • IELTS.org - Speaking Format
  • IELTS Liz - Speaking Tips and Lessons
  • IELTS Online Tests - Speaking Practice

The Three Parts of IELTS Speaking:

  1. Part 1 (4-5 minutes): Introduction and questions on familiar topics (home, family, work/study, hobbies, etc.)
  2. Part 2 (3-4 minutes): Individual long turn - You speak for 1-2 minutes on a given topic (with 1 minute preparation time)
  3. Part 3 (4-5 minutes): Two-way discussion - More abstract questions related to the Part 2 topic

Key Speaking Skills:

  • Fluency and coherence
  • Lexical resource (vocabulary)
  • Grammatical range and accuracy
  • Pronunciation

Practice Exercise: Speaking Part 1

Practice answering these common Part 1 questions. Record yourself if possible and listen back to evaluate your responses.

Topic: Home
  1. Where do you live? (city/town/village)
  2. Do you live in a house or an apartment?
  3. What do you like about your home?
  4. Is there anything you would like to change about your home?
Topic: Work/Study
  1. What do you do? (job/studies)
  2. Why did you choose this job/course?
  3. What do you enjoy most about your work/studies?
  4. What are your plans for the future?
Tips for Speaking Part 1:
  • Give more than just one-word or very short answers (aim for 2-3 sentences)
  • Use a variety of vocabulary and grammatical structures
  • Be conversational and natural
  • Don't memorize answers - the examiner can tell!
  • If you don't understand the question, ask for clarification
  • Speak clearly with good pronunciation
Sample Answer:

Question: Where do you live?

"I live in Auckland, which is the largest city in New Zealand. It's located in the North Island and is known for its beautiful harbors and multicultural environment. I've been living there for about three years now, and I really enjoy the lifestyle."

Question: What do you do?

"I'm currently working as an administrative assistant for a marketing company. My main responsibilities include managing the office schedule, handling correspondence, and assisting with client inquiries. I've been in this position for almost two years, and I find it quite rewarding because I get to interact with many different people every day."

Hour 2: General IELTS Writing Task 1 - Letter Writing

In General Training IELTS Writing Task 1, you need to write a letter of at least 150 words. You'll be given a situation and three points to include in your letter.

IELTS General Writing Task 1 Resources

Learn about letter writing for IELTS General Training:

  • British Council - General Writing Task 1
  • IELTS Liz - Letter Writing Tips
  • IELTS Advantage - Letter Writing Guide
  • IELTS Online Tests - Writing Practice

Types of IELTS Letters:

  1. Formal: To someone you don't know or in an official capacity
  2. Semi-formal: To someone you know in an official capacity
  3. Informal: To friends or family

Letter Structure:

  1. Opening: Address, date, and salutation (Dear...)
  2. Introduction: Purpose of the letter
  3. Body: Cover the three bullet points
  4. Conclusion: Final remarks and closing (Yours faithfully/sincerely, etc.)

Appropriate Salutations and Closings:

  • Formal:
    • Start: "Dear Sir/Madam" (if you don't know the name) or "Dear Mr./Ms./Dr. Smith" (if you know the name)
    • End: "Yours faithfully" (if you don't know the name) or "Yours sincerely" (if you know the name)
  • Informal:
    • Start: "Dear John," "Hi John," "Hello John,"
    • End: "Best wishes," "Kind regards," "Love," "Cheers,"

Practice Exercise: Writing a Formal Letter

Task: You've recently moved to a new apartment and have several problems with it. Write a letter to your landlord. In your letter:

  • Introduce yourself and state which apartment you're living in
  • Explain the problems you're having
  • Ask the landlord to resolve these issues

Write at least 150 words. You do NOT need to write any addresses.

Dear Mr. Anderson,


I am writing regarding apartment 503, which I moved into on 15th June. My name is Sarah Wilson, and I have signed a one-year lease for this property.


I would like to bring to your attention several issues that require immediate attention. Firstly, the heating system is not functioning properly, making the apartment very cold in the evenings. Secondly, there is a water leak under the kitchen sink that has damaged the cabinet. Finally, the washing machine makes an extremely loud noise when operating, which disturbs me and potentially my neighbors.


I would appreciate it if you could arrange for these problems to be fixed as soon as possible. I am available most evenings after 6 PM and all day on weekends if maintenance staff need access to the apartment. Please contact me at 555-7890 to arrange a suitable time.


Thank you for your prompt attention to these matters.


Yours sincerely,


Sarah Wilson

Notes on this letter:
  • This is a semi-formal letter as you know the name of the landlord
  • The introduction clearly states who you are and which apartment you're writing about
  • The three bullet points are addressed in separate sections
  • The letter includes specific details about the problems
  • The conclusion includes a clear request and contact information
  • The letter is 174 words (above the minimum 150 words)

Hour 3: Vocabulary Building & Practice

In this hour, we'll focus on building your vocabulary for both speaking and writing, with particular emphasis on useful phrases for letter writing.

Useful Phrases for Letter Writing:

Formal/Semi-formal Letters:
  • Opening purpose:
    • I am writing to inquire about...
    • I am writing with regard to...
    • I am writing to express my concern about...
    • I am writing to complain about...
  • Making requests:
    • I would be grateful if you could...
    • I would appreciate it if you could...
    • Could you possibly...
    • I was wondering if it would be possible to...
  • Closing remarks:
    • Thank you for your assistance in this matter.
    • I look forward to hearing from you soon.
    • I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter.
    • Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require further information.
Informal Letters:
  • Opening purpose:
    • I'm writing to let you know that...
    • Just a quick note about...
    • I thought I'd drop you a line to...
    • Hope you're well. I'm writing because...
  • Making requests:
    • Could you possibly...
    • I was hoping you might be able to...
    • I'd really appreciate it if you could...
    • Do you think you could...
  • Closing remarks:
    • Thanks for your help.
    • Looking forward to hearing from you.
    • Let me know what you think.
    • Write back soon!

Practice Exercise: Writing an Informal Letter

Task: You're going on vacation and need someone to look after your pet. Write a letter to a friend asking for help. In your letter:

  • Explain your plans and when you'll be away
  • Describe what looking after your pet involves
  • Suggest how you could return the favor

Write at least 150 words.

Hi Jamie,


Hope you're doing well! I'm writing to ask for a huge favor. I've just booked a holiday to Thailand from August 15th to 29th, and I'm looking for someone to take care of my cat, Luna, while I'm away.


Looking after Luna is fairly straightforward. She needs feeding twice a day - in the morning and evening. Her food is in the kitchen cupboard above the sink, and she only needs one sachet per meal. Her litter box would need cleaning every couple of days, and she'd love some playtime whenever you have a spare moment. She's very friendly and loves company!


I'd be happy to return the favor anytime you need it. I'm also bringing back some Thai souvenirs for you, and I'd be more than happy to treat you to dinner at that new Italian restaurant you mentioned when I get back.


Let me know if this would be possible. I could drop my spare keys off next week if you're available.


Thanks a million!


Cheers,

Alex

Notes on this letter:
  • This is an informal letter to a friend
  • The tone is friendly and conversational
  • All three bullet points are addressed in separate paragraphs
  • Specific details are included about the pet care requirements
  • The conclusion includes a clear follow-up action
  • The letter is 174 words (above the minimum 150 words)
