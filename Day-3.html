Hour 1: IELTS Listening Section Types

The IELTS Listening test consists of four sections, each with a different focus and increasing difficulty. Let's look at each in detail:

The Four Listening Sections:

Section 1:
  • Format: A conversation between two people in an everyday social context
  • Common scenarios: Accommodation inquiries, job applications, service inquiries
  • Question types: Form completion, multiple choice, matching
  • Focus on: Personal information, numbers, dates, times, locations
  • Difficulty level: Easiest of the four sections
Section 2:
  • Format: A monologue set in an everyday social context
  • Common scenarios: Tour guide information, facility descriptions, event announcements
  • Question types: Multiple choice, map/plan/diagram labeling, form completion
  • Focus on: Factual information about places, events, or services
  • Difficulty level: Moderate
Section 3:
  • Format: A conversation between up to four people in an educational or training context
  • Common scenarios: University tutorials, project discussions, academic advisement
  • Question types: Multiple choice, matching, note completion
  • Focus on: Educational topics, academic discussions, student experiences
  • Difficulty level: Challenging
Section 4:
  • Format: A monologue on an academic subject
  • Common scenarios: University lectures, academic presentations
  • Question types: Sentence completion, multiple choice, summary completion
  • Focus on: Abstract academic concepts, detailed information
  • Difficulty level: Most difficult section
